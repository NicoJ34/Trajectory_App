## Proposition de Valeur

### Core Value Proposition
> **Trajectory transforms how intermediate runners prepare for their races. By intelligently adapting your training plan to your performance, life circumstances, and recovery signals—without requiring a personal coach—Trajectory becomes your trusted companion from training start to race day.**

### Unique Selling Points (USPs)

✅ **Adaptive Planning** — Plans evolve weekly based on your performance, not static schedules  
✅ **Context-Aware** — You signal constraints (illness, travel, equipment limits) and the plan adjusts automatically  
✅ **Multi-Race Support** — Plan multiple races in your calendar; Trajectory chains them seamlessly  
✅ **Flexible Dates** — Your plan extends to your race date (minimum 8 weeks), not a fixed duration  
✅ **Holistic Training** — Integrated cross-training (strength, swimming, cycling, hiking) woven into one coherent plan  
✅ **No Coach Required** — Professional-grade training intelligence, accessible to all

## Positioning

### Market Position
**The intelligent, adaptive training companion for intermediate runners preparing for 10K–marathon distances.**

### Who We Are For
- Intermediate runners (1-3 years consistent training)
- Training for 10K, semi-marathon, or marathon
- Want structure + flexibility (life happens)
- Don't want—or can't afford—a personal coach
- Want their plan to adapt to them, not vice versa

### Who We're NOT For
- Beginners (need foundational guidance)
- Ultra-marathon specialists (>42km distances, out of scope for MVP)
- Athletes with coach (already coached)
- Casual joggers (no specific race goal)

### Competitive Landscape
**vs. Static Plans (Strava, Nike Run Club):** Trajectory adapts; they don't  
**vs. Personal Coaching:** Trajectory is affordable, always available; coaching is expensive, limited availability  
**vs. AI-Heavy Apps (TrainAsONE):** Trajectory feels personal + context-aware; many AI apps feel black-box

## Tone of Voice

### Core Personality Traits

| Trait | Definition | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Intelligent** | Informed, knowledgeable about running science | "Your body's signals tell us when to push and when to recover." |
| **Adaptive** | Flexible, responsive, understanding | "Travel for work? Let's adjust your week." |
| **Supportive** | Encouraging, non-judgmental, empathetic | "Sometimes the best run is the one you needed, not the one planned." |
| **Grounded** | Practical, transparent, no hype | "We won't promise you a PR. We'll give you a smarter plan." |
| **Clear** | Simple language, jargon-free when possible | Use "easy run" not "Zone 2 base-building stimulus" |

### Tone Examples

**When the user skips a session:**
✅ "Life happens. Let's adjust next week based on what you did."  
❌ "You failed to complete today's workout."

**When detecting fatigue:**
✅ "Your performance suggests you're tired. How about we recover this week and come back stronger?"  
❌ "System alert: Overtraining detected."

**When introducing a new feature:**
✅ "You can now tell us about your week—travel, illness, new location—and we'll adapt."  
❌ "Constraint input mechanism now live."

## Key Differentiators

### What Makes Trajectory Different

| Feature | Trajectory | Competitors |
|---------|-----------|---|
| **Adaptation Trigger** | Performance + context | Usually performance only |
| **Context Input** | Users signal constraints | Auto-detection (unreliable) |
| **Multi-Race Support** | Chain races seamlessly | Single race focus |
| **Plan Duration** | Extends to race date | Fixed 8-12 weeks |
| **Cross-Training** | Integrated into main plan | Separate, optional |
| **Recovery Signals** | Performance + user feedback | HR metrics only |

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Context-Aware** — Only app that lets users signal real-life changes
2. **Flexible Duration** — Plans fit YOUR timeline, not a template
3. **Holistic** — Cross-training isn't an afterthought; it's core
4. **Approachable** — AI that feels personal, not robotic
5. **Accessible** — Coach-quality training without coach price

## Brand Voice in Action

### Email Example
**Subject:** You crushed it this week. Here's what's next.

> Hey [Name],
>
> We loved your 5-miler on Thursday. Your pace was strong and consistent—exactly where we want you for marathon prep. And we saw you took Sunday as a full rest day. Smart call.
>
> This coming week, we're bumping up your long run by 1km to 16km. You've earned it. We'll keep tempo intensity the same to let your body adapt gradually.
>
> One thing: We noticed you skipped the strength session last week. That's okay (life happens!), but we'd love to see you get 1-2 sessions in this week if possible. Stronger legs = stronger race.
>
> Ready for next week?
>
> —Trajectory

### In-App Notification Example

**When user logs a hard run with high fatigue:**

> "Solid effort today. Your pace was strong, but RPE was high. We're watching for signs of overtraining. If you feel it next week, just let us know—we'll scale back."

**When auto-detecting a drop in performance:**

> "Your pace dipped about 5% this week. That's normal. Could be fatigue, could be life stress, could be just an off day. Give us a signal if something's up, and we'll adjust."

## Usage Guidelines

### DO

✅ Use "Trajectory" in all official communications  
✅ Emphasize adaptation, flexibility, intelligence  
✅ Focus on the runner's experience, not the tech  
✅ Show diverse runners in diverse situations  
✅ Be encouraging and realistic (no false promises)  
✅ Use "we" when speaking on behalf of the app  

### DON'T

❌ Use "Planner" or "Planning" as a standalone reference; always frame within Trajectory  
❌ Oversell; avoid "revolutionary," "game-changing," hype  
❌ Use technical jargon (adaptive algorithms, ML, etc.) in user-facing copy  
❌ Make guarantees about performance/PRs (we adapt plans, not physics)  
❌ Position as a replacement for medical advice or professional coaching  
❌ Use generic running imagery (stock photos of happy runners in perfect conditions)

## Internal Alignment

### For Product Team
Trajectory is about listening and responding. Every feature should enhance the adaptive loop:  
Input (user tells us something) → Algorithm (we think about it) → Output (we adjust the plan)

### For Design Team
Trajectory feels intelligent but not intimidating. Clean, modern, with warmth. The UI should be a conversation, not a dashboard.

### For Marketing Team
Lead with the pain point: Static plans don't adapt. Lead with the solution: Yours does. Lead with proof: Real runners, real results.

### For Support Team
Trajectory listens. When users contact us, we listen too. Their feedback informs plan adjustments. Every interaction is part of the adaptive loop.
